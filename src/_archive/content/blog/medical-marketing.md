Marketing a medical practice is different. You are not only selling a service — you are asking for trust. Whether you are a [plastic surgeon](/solutions/plastic-surgeons) targeting procedure research queries or a [med spa](/solutions/med-spas) promoting injectables, your SEO strategy must be precise and defensible.

## 1. Your Money or Your Life (YMYL)

Google treats medical content under YMYL guidelines. The bar for quality is higher. Generic filler content will not cut it. You need to demonstrate **experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust (E-E-A-T)**.

- **Author bios:** Clinical or editorial review by qualified professionals where appropriate.
- **Citations:** Link to reputable sources (for example, NIH, PubMed) when stating clinical facts.
- **Safety and expectations:** Be transparent about risks, recovery, and candidacy — this also converts better-qualified patients.

Our [E-E-A-T optimizer](/platform/features/eeat-optimizer) module is built to surface gaps in trust signals and on-page structure — a useful checklist alongside medical board and regional advertising rules.

## 2. Local SEO for patients

Patients want convenience. They search for "Botox near me" or "best dermatologist in [city]". Your Google Business Profile must be accurate: photos of the facility, updated hours, and review responses that respect privacy and compliance.

## 3. The power of "before and after" galleries

For aesthetic practices, results are persuasive — but they must be optimized responsibly.

- **Alt text:** Describe the procedure and view (for example, "Rhinoplasty before and after, side profile").
- **File names:** Prefer descriptive names over `IMG_123.jpg`.
- **Performance:** Compress images so galleries do not tank [Core Web Vitals](/platform/features/technical-seo-audits).
